# Abstract

Single Event Upsets (SEU) on TDRS-1 correlates with space environment Ground based neutron monitor data illustrates relationship between galactic cosmic rays (GCR) and TDRS-1 upsets. Space enviroment data from GOES-7

# A. Introduction

Tracking and Data Relay Satellite System (TDRSS) from NASA to provide comms for LEO satellites. TDRS-1 just short of geosynchronous orbit due to launching failure but then fixes with onboard thrusters. During this fix the first anomaly was observed in Attitude Control System (ACS) on the Random Access Memory (RAM) - now known as SEU. 

SEU changes the state of memeory device through energitic particle penetration and deposition energy along its path. charge deposition $\propto Z/v^2$  , $Z$ = Atomic number, $v$ is speed.

# B. Recording SEUs on TDRS-1
ACS contains four pages of RAM, 256 bytes per page consiting of two static bi-olar Fiarchild 93L422 (256 x 4 bit) RAM chips. Half of the total RAM is in use

Used RAM has check sum algorithm, unused monitors through RAM dumps.

Quantitative threat to the TDRS-1 mission "In all 8 chips there are 42 bits in normal mode that will cause sun/earth pointing loss (tumble) if left unattended. Some must be answered in less than 90 seconds"

# C. Energetic Particles in Near-Earth Space

Three sources of particle radiation in space environment
1. Galactic Cosmic Rays (GCRs)
2. Energetic Solar Particles from solar flares
3. Energetic particles from radiation belts from Earths magnetosphere

# D. GCRs and TDRS-1 SEUs

Range of energy for GCR: several MeV to $10^{14}$ MeV. Consists of mainly protons and $\alpha$ particles. GCR flux maximum at solar minimum and minimum at solar maximum. Solar activity shields  against GCR.

Depressions in GCR flux known as Forbush Decreases resulting from interplanetary disturbances produced by impulsive solar events

# E. Energetic Solar Particles and TDRS-1 SEUs

Large solar flares cause a substantial response in the TDRS-1 SUE rate. associated with Ground Level Events (GLE)s indicating high energy component to flux.

Solar Particle Event measured by GOES-7. Normad and Stapor prove primary cause of TDRS-1 SEUs during solar flares

SEU panel shows upsets that resulted in either operational anomalies or Page 2 check sum errors.

# F. SEUs on Subsequent TDRS

On subsequent TDRS satellites RAM was upgraded leaving no reported SEUs onboard. 

High values for effective Linear Energy Transfer (LET) threshold represnts low SEU sensitivity. High values for the device upset cross section indicate high SEU sensitivity
