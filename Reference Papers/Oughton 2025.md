# Abstract

 **What will NASAs future communication demand and expenditure look like, in a paradigm where space policy encourages maximum private sector involvement?** NASA will rely more on commercial procurement for Direct - To - Earth and Space Relay communication service.  Developed a new Techno - Economic Assessment (TEA) model to capture costs overtime using private services.  Using private services could reduce cost with a tradeoff of of contracting competition and excessive pricing.
# Introduction

NASA provides network communication and tracking services via the Space Communications and Navigation Program (SCaN) for missions (formed in 2006). On it are two networks
* Deeps Space Network (DSN)
	* Government Owned and Contractor Operated (GOCO)
	* operates >2 million km from earth
* Near- Space Network (NSN)
	* Blended GOCO networks
	* Space/Ground relays
	* < 2 million km
SCaN has evolved since early space program providing comms for  Mercury, Gemini, and Apollo era missions. Human continual presence on ISS required more reliable and robust comms. 

NASA implemented a space based architecture between 1938 and 1995 with 1st gen of Tracking and Data Relay Satellites (TDRS).

NASA as shifted from a GOCO model involving paying for antennas and infrastructure eventually operated by commercial companies, to NASA directly purchasing provided communication and tracking services (costed in passes or minutes) from commercially-owned and -operated infrastructure.

Space domain being greatly invested in modern times with focus on space launch vehicles, new satellites and enritre constellations. $546 billion worth now expected to be $800 billion in future. With Artemis program sight on Mars we need rourdes to ensure government spending if focused on areas of market failures. Emerging use case for 6G.

SCaN effort to a fully commerical model begain in 2018 with 8 potential commerical partners. this formed the Communications Services Project (CSP) that will work with the commerical partners to demonstrate and evaluate satellite comm capability.

Questions to be answered:
1. What might current and future NASA mission demand look like (RQ1)?
2. Given the various network options available, what are the costs of production in relation to potential demand (RQ2)? 
3. For different market scenarios, what is the cost if all current and future missions are shifted to commercial services (RQ3)?
